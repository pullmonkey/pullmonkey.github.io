I got my headlight/taillight form fat tire cycles on montono. Working pretty well, they are more for others to see me than they are for improving visibility.

And, I’ve been reading tons about cadence, either being a spinner or a masher, like Armstrong or Ulrich. I have tried both so far. For Monday’s trip home, I used a low cadence of 60 rpm and it took me 10 percent longer than Tuesday’s ride where I increased my cadence to an average of 90 rpm. It is interesting that increasing my cadence by 50% only improved my time by 10%, except I wasn’t as worn out at the end. What I noticed was that my legs weren’t as tired and I was able to get up those hills more easily. It definitely seems worth messing with cadence to find the right fit, so I am going to try a few more things.
